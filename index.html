Australia's Renewable Energy Dashboard

Exploring Australia’s renewable transition through state-level growth, national trends, and emission outcomes (2010–2025).

The story of Australia’s clean energy transition begins here across its vast and varied landscape. In 2010, only a few states flickered with renewable generation, their contributions modest and scattered. As the years roll forward, the map lights up bubbles swell and shift south to north, tracing the nation’s accelerating commitment to clean power. By 2025, what once were isolated sparks of progress have expanded into a network of thriving renewable regions, marking a national transformation-built state by state.

Zooming out to the national scale, two lines tell the heart of Australia’s energy story one climbing steadily upward, the other falling away. The red line of emissions begins high, reflecting the country’s coal-heavy grid. But as the green line of renewables surges forward, the two begin to diverge a visual turning point showing how clean energy isn’t just growing; it’s actively replacing what came before. By 2025, the distance between them speaks louder than numbers ever could a decade of transition reshaping the way Australia powers its future.

Behind the national trend lie six distinct stories, each state navigating its own path toward sustainability. This heatmap captures that evolution cool tones in 2010 give way to deep greens by 2025, revealing how momentum spread outward from early leaders like South Australia and Tasmania. Each square marks progress, but together, they paint a pattern of persistence: policy shifts, investment, and innovation converging across the nation. By the end of the timeline, nearly every state contributes meaningfully proving that Australia’s renewable rise is no longer regional, but collective.

The journey concludes where cause meets effect. Each point in this scatterplot is a year in time and together, they trace a clear and powerful relationship. As renewable share climbs, emission intensity falls in lockstep, revealing the true impact of the transformation. What begins as scattered uncertainty in early years becomes a tight, downward path evidence that clean energy isn’t just a choice, but a measurable driver of Australia’s decarbonisation success.
